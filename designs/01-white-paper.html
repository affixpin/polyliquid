I.

The oracle problem

Prediction markets need oracles to determine event outcomes. For subjective events — politics, economics, culture — automated feeds like Chainlink cannot help. Human-powered oracles are required.

UMA, the dominant oracle, uses token-weighted voting. Influence equals token holdings. A whale can purchase control in a single transaction. The system has been exploited repeatedly.

II.

Three layers of security

Delegation, consensus, and dispute resolution.
1

LP Delegation

Liquidity providers choose voters by track record and delegate capital. Commission: 5–80%. Higher reputation means lower risk for LPs.

2

Commit-Reveal

Voters submit encrypted votes, then reveal. Weight = Stake × Reputation. Consensus at 50% + 1. 99% of markets resolve in 8 hours.

3

DAO Dispute

Traders dispute with a bond equal to 2% of market size. DAO votes over 48 hours. Attackers lose everything.
