# Loom Demo Script — 2 min

## [0:00–0:20] Open on hero
*(Cursor resting on hero section)*

> "So this is Polyliquid — a decentralized reputational oracle for prediction markets. The core idea is simple: vote weight equals stake times reputation. You can't buy reputation. You earn it over months of correct resolutions. And that one property changes everything about oracle security."

## [0:20–0:40] Scroll down through the-split
*(Slow scroll to the comparison cards)*

> "Let me show you what I mean. On the left — how token-weighted oracles work today. You buy tokens, you vote, you profit, you sell. Total cost: around $750 plus $2.5M in tokens. On the right — Polyliquid. Even with $10M, if you have no reputation, your influence is zero. The attack cost exceeds the market itself."

## [0:40–1:00] Continue scrolling through how-it-works, then click into /vaults
*(Scroll past the three layers, then click "Explore Vaults")*

> "We achieve this through three layers — LP delegation, commit-reveal voting where LPs can actually pull out if they see something suspicious, and DAO arbitration as a final backstop. But let me show you what this looks like in practice."

*(Now on /vaults page)*

> "This is the voter marketplace. LPs come here, pick a voter based on their track record — reputation, accuracy, APR — and delegate capital. Commission is set by the free market, anywhere from 0 to 99%."

## [1:00–1:25] Click into a vault, then /activity
*(Click on Apex Oracle)*

> "Inside a vault you see everything — how much is staked, the commission, recent resolutions. An LP just deposits USDC and earns yield. Their capital backs honest voting."

*(Click to /activity)*

> "And here's the resolution feed. Every market, every vote, fully transparent. You can expand any event and see exactly who voted what, with what weight, and whether consensus was reached. Nothing hidden."

## [1:25–1:50] Back to landing, scroll to economics and roadmap
*(Click logo to go home, scroll to economics)*

> "Now — why would a voter never cheat? Because on dispute, they lose everything. 100% of their capital, 100% of their reputation, all future income — gone. Recovery takes about a year. The math says attacking costs 219% of the market. With DAO arbitration on top, it's infinite. There's simply no scenario where cheating pays."

*(Scroll to roadmap)*

> "We're starting the testnet pilot now in Q2, going live with subjective markets through Q3, and then opening up fully permissionless market creation."

## [1:50–2:00] Close
*(Scroll back up to hero or stay on roadmap)*

> "That's Polyliquid. Scales to any number of markets, zero cost to the platform, and the only oracle where trust is earned — not bought."

## Tips
- Talk like you're showing this to a friend, not presenting
- Let the UI breathe — pause 1–2 seconds on each page before talking
- Don't rush the vault detail — hover over the deposit panel to show it's real
- End looking at camera if possible for the closing line
